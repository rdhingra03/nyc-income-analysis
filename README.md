# nyc-income-analysis
R-based analysis of income inequality in New York City using Housing and Vacancy Survey data. Includes exploratory data analysis, regression modeling, and evaluation of key predictors such as age, housing conditions, and time in the city.
Key Findings
- Maintenance deficiencies have a significant negative relationship with income
- Age and year moved to NYC are not strong predictors
- Model is statistically significant but has low explanatory power (R² ≈ 0.06)

## Example Prediction
Predicted income for a 53-year-old who moved to NYC in 1987 with 3 maintenance deficiencies:
